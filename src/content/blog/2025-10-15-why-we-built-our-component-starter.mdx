The modern web has a weight problem. Average page sizes have ballooned past 2MB, and most of that weight serves no one — not the user, not the brand, not the business.

At Prism Studio, we take a different approach. We call it **static-first design** — and it starts with a simple question: *what can this page be without JavaScript?*

The answer, more often than not, is: nearly everything.

## The case for going lean

When you build with semantic HTML and purposeful CSS, you get pages that load in under a second. You get experiences that work on every device, every connection speed, every browser. You get accessibility baked in from the start.

That is not a compromise — it is a superpower.

Our stack of choice is Astro, which renders everything to static HTML by default. When we need interactivity, we add it surgically. A carousel here. A form validation there. Never a 200KB framework for a toggle button.

## What this means for our clients

Faster sites convert better. This is not opinion — it is data. Every 100ms of latency costs conversions. When we deliver sites that load in 400ms instead of 4 seconds, the business impact is immediate and measurable.

But performance is just the beginning. Static-first sites are also:

- **More secure** — no server-side vulnerabilities
- **More reliable** — CDN-hosted, globally distributed
- **More maintainable** — less code, fewer moving parts
- **More sustainable** — lower energy consumption per pageview

## The craft behind the speed

Going fast does not mean cutting corners. Our static sites feature rich animations, fluid layouts, and careful typographic details. We just achieve them with CSS instead of JavaScript. With custom properties instead of runtime theming. With native browser APIs instead of polyfill layers.

The result is work that feels premium and loads instantly. That is the Prism Studio promise.
